Inside the vibrant world of Blackpink's Born Pink tour

Creative director Amy Bowerman collaborated with the K-pop group for their biggest world tour yet.

Going to a K-pop concert is an attack on the senses in the best way possible. Sound is obviously well covered. For sight, there's meticulously-crafted choreography and costumes. Even touch is ticked off in the form of ‘lightsticks’, band-specific batons that you wave to form a light show.

“All of the elements within the show [were] based around Pink Venom,” says Amy Bowerman, whose role covers everything from stage design to visual storytelling.

Since debuting in 2016, Blackpink have solidified themselves as one of the most notable K-pop acts, breaking barriers by eschewing stereotypes for a more transgressive take on femininity. Based on the title of their second album, the tour, which kicked off in Seoul, South Korea, saw members Jisoo, Jennie, Rosé, and Lisa perform in front of millions around the world.

The show comes in the shape of moving stages, blinding pyrotechnics, confetti cannons, and enormous “blow-through” video screens. Capturing the multiplicity of Blackpink was a challenge, Bowerman says, as “their songs are very aggressive, but then they have some songs that feel very smooth, sexy, and feminine.”

Bowerman says the band themselves are meticulous about how the concert plays out. “They're very hands-on with their music and if they don't hear something that they like, they will just go back into the studio and rewrite that piece of music. It's a full family affair of everybody getting involved.”
