Lomo Saltado

Lomo saltado is a traditional Peruvian dish, a stir-fry that typically combines marinated strips of sirloin (or other beef steak) with onions, tomatoes, french fries, and other ingredients, and is typically served with rice. The dish originated as part of the chifa tradition, the Chinese cuisine of Peru, though its popularity has made it part of the mainstream culture.

  • Prep time20 mins
  • Cook time15 mins
  • Servings4 People

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup red wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp ají amarillo
  • 1 lb beef tenderloin, cut into ½-inch strips
  • Adobo seasoning with pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large red onion, sliced
  • 2 tsp minced garlic
  • ½ bag (28 oz.) frozen French fries
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced into strips
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 cups extra-long grain rice

Steps

  1. Whisk vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, and yellow chili paste in a small bowl.
  2. Sear the beef strips in a hot pan until browned. Remove and set aside.
  3. Sauté onions and garlic in the same pan until softened.
  4. Combine: Return beef to the pan, add the sauce, fold in fries and tomatoes, garnish with parsley, and serve over rice.
