Lomo Saltado

Lomo Saltado is a traditional Peruvian stir-fry that combines marinated strips of sirloin with onions, tomatoes, and french fries, served over rice.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup red wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp yellow chili paste (ají amarillo)
  • 1 lb beef tenderloin, cut into ½-inch strips
  • Adobo seasoning with pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large red onion, sliced into ¼-inch strips
  • 2 tsp minced garlic
  • ½ bag (28 oz.) frozen French fries
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced into strips
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 cups extra-long grain rice

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, mix the vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, and yellow chili paste. Set aside.
  2. Season the beef with adobo. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a skillet. Add beef and cook for 3 minutes until browned. Remove and set aside.
  3. Heat remaining oil, add onions, and cook until soft. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds.
  4. Return beef to skillet, add vinegar mixture, fries, and tomatoes. Toss to coat and serve with rice.
